SC04 Gradient of Tangent Practice Question

The gradient of the tangent to the curve $y = x^2 - 4x + 3$ is $2$. Find the coordinates of the point on the curve where this occurs.

📌 考点：Gradient of Tangent · 难度：基础

📌 Topic: Gradient of Tangent · Difficulty: 基础

✅ 答案

✅ Answer

Let $y = x^2 - 4x + 3$. \\ Differentiating with respect to $x$, we get $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 4$. \\ We are given that the gradient is $2$, so we set $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2$: \\ $2x - 4 = 2$ \\ $2x = 6$ \\ $x = 3$. \\ To find the $y$-coordinate, substitute $x = 3$ into the original equation: \\ $y = (3)^2 - 4(3) + 3 = 9 - 12 + 3 = 0$. \\ The coordinates of the point are $(3, 0)$.
